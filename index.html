        About this map

        Each dot is a paper, positioned by a 2D UMAP of its text embedding, so papers with similar text sit near each other. Use Embedding model to switch the embedding space (e.g. SPECTER2 vs Gemini) — positions and topics change, the citation graph stays the same. By default colors mark BERTopic topics; you can switch to Leiden communities, year, or citation count. Dot size is total citation degree.

        Hover any paper for its title, year and authors. Click a paper to draw its citation links and open the side panel. Toggle citation edges to see where the citation graph crosses the semantic map — long edges connect papers cited together but semantically distant.

        Intra-topic citation islands asks a sharper question: within a single topic, do the papers form one connected citation body or several disconnected ones? It draws only citations inside each topic and finds the connected components of that subgraph. The largest body keeps the topic color; separate communities (≥2 papers) get vivid colors and are enlarged (they often overlap the main body in semantic space); lone papers that cite nothing within the topic are greyed. Click a topic to read its breakdown — what fraction of the topic sits in the main body vs. disconnected islands vs. isolated papers.
